# Proposal Boilerplate: Heartland for Children Reference

Quotes from an unscripted 2024 interview with the Director of Program Operations at Heartland for Children. Organized by the point you need to make. Drop these into proposals, capability statements, RFP responses, and sales conversations.

---

## When you need to show you understand mission-critical work

Heartland for Children serves over 1,500 children at any given time across Polk, Highlands, and Hardee counties. Their work involves foster care, adoption, independent living, and an array of services for children and families in the child welfare system. They contract with the Florida Department of Children and Families. The compliance and reporting requirements are not optional.

> "We have so many programs, very complex programs, within our system of care. We cover a three-county geographic area. We have policy and procedure that we must follow by the Department of Children and Families. There's a lot of tracking involved, needing to know where things are exactly in a process in order to meet those guidelines."

---

## When the prospect is still patching things together

> "We were using very legacy Microsoft databases. Those databases now are almost non-existent. They're no longer supported by Microsoft. The coding was very old. We were getting to the point that it was causing glitches in the system, could possibly cause a loss of data."

> "We were doing a lot of manual spreadsheet tracking. The reporting was very manual, sometimes taking multiple spreadsheets, having to crosswalk those data spreadsheets. Because it was such a labor-intensive process, there were a lot of reporting we could not even get to, because we just did not have the manpower."

> "The Rudolph Roundup system was an Access database. It was literally standing on one leg. Only one person could be in it. It was locking up. We could only put limited information in there because it had been used for years."

## When they're worried about the process being disruptive

> "I felt Mosier Data was very empowered in really seeing the outcomes, like really feeling like they were traveling this with us."

## When they're concerned about training and adoption

> "There really wasn't a training that was needed because you were learning the entire time. As you were building the system, as you were testing the system, there really wasn't a need for training. Because by the time you got done, you knew how to run the system."

> "If there was a question at any point, we could shoot an email or a phone call to have that resolved."

## When you need measurable ROI

> "We were able to reduce our services by 15 percent."

> "We are able to get home studies done probably in half the time we were getting them done before."

> "For Rudolph Roundup, it has saved enormous hours of time, of manpower. If we did not have the system to support Rudolph Roundup, it would take us triple the man hours to run this project."

> "Before, we did not have time for reporting. That was just impossible time-wise. Where now, we not only run monthly reports, we run weekly reports."

> "We now can see that right on our dashboard. We don't even have to run reporting. Our services are broken out into probably about 20 different categories. At any time, we can look on our dashboard and we know exactly how many services we have in every single one of those categories."

## When they ask why clients come back

Heartland for Children first engaged us for Rudolph Roundup, their annual gift-matching event. A year later, they came back with a more complex need: tracking children placed across county and state lines with full compliance reporting.

> "Because of how well the first database went, how easy it was to work with Mosier, we knew then at that point all the possibilities that we could do with this other very complex process that we had."
