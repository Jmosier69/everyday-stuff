# Heartland for Children: What They Said

Nikki, Director of Program Operations at Heartland for Children, sat down with us after two systems and roughly two years of working together. These are her words, organized by theme. We didn't script anything. We just asked questions and let her talk.

---

## On what it was like before

> "We were using very legacy Microsoft databases. Those databases now are almost non-existent. They're no longer supported by Microsoft. The coding was very old. We were getting to the point that it was causing glitches in the system, could possibly cause a loss of data."

> "We were doing a lot of manual spreadsheet tracking. The reporting was very manual, sometimes taking multiple spreadsheets, having to crosswalk those data spreadsheets. Because it was such a labor-intensive process, there were a lot of reporting we could not even get to, because we just did not have the manpower."

> "It was almost like you were busy putting out fires because you really couldn't track. Well, we have a service out. Was someone assigned? Were they not assigned yet? Because of the volume of services, it made it even harder."

> "The Rudolph Roundup system was an Access database. It was literally standing on one leg. We went from an Access database where only one person could be in it. It was locking up."

---

## On working with us

> "I felt Mosier Data was very empowered in really seeing the outcomes, like really feeling like they were traveling this with us. Very open to everything that we were saying and needing, really trying to understand it."

> "If we happen to notice that we changed our mind on something, it was not a big deal. Again, we could just bring that to the table and work through it."

> "The rollout went so smooth. There were a couple of minor tweaks, but nothing that delayed the rollout of that project. So it was very successful."

> "There really wasn't a training that was needed because you were learning the entire time. As you were building the system, as you were testing the system, there really wasn't a need for training. Because by the time you got done, you knew how to run the system."

---

## On the results

> "We were able to reduce our services by 15 percent. And also, it definitely saved manpower. Before, we did not have time for reporting. That was just impossible time-wise. Where now, we not only run monthly reports, we run weekly reports."

> "We are able to get home studies done probably in half the time we were getting them done before."

> "For Rudolph Roundup, it has saved enormous hours of time, of manpower. If we did not have the system to support Rudolph Roundup, it would take us triple the man hours to run this project."

> "We now can see that right on our dashboard. We don't even have to run reporting. Our services are broken out into probably about 20 different categories. At any time, we can look on our dashboard and we know exactly how many services we have in every single one of those categories."

---
